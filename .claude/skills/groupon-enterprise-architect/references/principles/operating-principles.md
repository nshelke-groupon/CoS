Groupon operates under a high-performance model. These five principles are actionable standards — not slogans. Every decision, action, and interaction is filtered through them. They drive culture, performance cadence, and delivery.

---

## The Five Principles

### 1. Extreme Ownership

**One owner per outcome; no passengers, no excuses.**

Every individual operates as a true accountable owner — not a committee, not a team, one person. This eliminates ambiguity, centralizes authority, and ensures results never fall between the cracks.

- **Execution:** End-to-end delivery. No gap without a clear owner.
- **Decisions:** Trade for impact, accountability, and clarity — not comfort.
- **Culture:** High trust, direct accountability across peers.

### 2. Speed Over Comfort

**Ship fast, learn faster. Perfect is too slow.**

Progress over perfection. No waiting for learning plans, formalities, or consensus. Faster cycles make long debates and slow execution prohibitively expensive.

- **Execution:** Bias to start and finish — just ship it.
- **Decisions:** Act with adequate data, expect results. Do not wait for certainty.
- **Culture:** Celebrate progress and learning, not process compliance alone.

### 3. Impact Obsessed

**Focus only on what moves the metrics that matter.**

Prioritize measurable outcomes. The org does not chase perception or effort — only what truly drives business results.

- **Execution:** Resources only flow to outcomes, not ideas.
- **Decisions:** Prioritize metrics that track growth or profitability.
- **Culture:** Celebrate results over effort. Reviews are data-driven.

### 4. Simplify to Scale

**Complexity kills. Simplicity scales.**

Simplicity is a force multiplier. Complexity slows execution, confuses clarity, and fragments teams. Simplification creates space for efficiency.

- **Execution:** Choose the simplest path that solves 80% of value.
- **Decisions:** Prioritize clarity, not cleverness. Reduce layers and roles.
- **Culture:** Fewer meetings, shorter docs, flatter structures.

### 5. Disciplined

**Do more with less; constraints breed creativity.**

Quality, consistency, and sustainable growth through resource discipline. Use resources wisely, maintain standards, hold a high bar — even at low or no cost.

- **Execution:** Efficient use of time, capital, and technology.
- **Culture:** Pride in resourcefulness. Smart spending as a strength.

## Key Metrics

The principles are measured against business outcomes:

- **Unit economics** — profitability per transaction
- **Customer acquisition costs** — efficiency of growth spend
- **Gross profit / income** — top-line health
- **LTV/CAC** — long-term customer value vs. acquisition cost

Every initiative should connect to at least one of these metrics. If it does not move a metric that matters, question whether it should exist.

## Leadership Standards

Leadership accountability includes operating as a role model for these principles:

- **Results-driven** — outcomes over activity
- **People-focused** — coaching, mentoring, enabling autonomy
- **Removing blockers** — clearing the path for teams to execute
- **Creating accountability cycles** — feedback based on results, not intent
- **Delegating with trust** — ownership pushed down, not hoarded

Leaders are expected to discuss the principles actively in standups, retrospectives, feedback sessions, decision-making, and recruiting. This is an operating guide, not a poster.

## Application for Architecture Decisions

When evaluating architecture proposals, apply these principles as filters:

1. **Extreme Ownership** — Does the design have one clear owner per service/domain?
2. **Speed Over Comfort** — Can we ship an MVP in weeks, not months?
3. **Impact Obsessed** — Does this directly improve a key metric?
4. **Simplify to Scale** — Is this the simplest design that solves 80% of the need?
5. **Disciplined** — Are we using existing infrastructure before buying/building new?
