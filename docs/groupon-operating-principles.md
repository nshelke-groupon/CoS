# Groupon Operating Principles 2026

> Source: [Groupon Team Hub — Operating Principles](https://sites.google.com/groupon.com/groupon-team-hub/2026-strategy/operating-principles)
> Captured: 2026-03-01

In 2026, Groupon is moving to a high-performance operating model designed to support our next phase of transformation. These Operating Principles define the behaviors, decision-making standards, and execution expectations that every teammate follows regardless of role, level, or location.

They are not slogans or aspirations — they are actionable standards. Every decision, every action, and every interaction should be filtered through these principles, and we expect them to be used actively, not passively. These principles drive our culture, our performance cadence, and how we deliver on our Company Role.

The five beliefs behind how our company operates, a FAQ is available for all teammates.

## The Five Principles

### 1. Extreme Ownership

**One owner per outcome; no passengers, no excuses.**

Extreme Ownership means every individual operates like a true accountable owner, not a committee, not a team/unit, just one "the owner." This principle eliminates ambiguity, centralizes authority, and ensures that results never fall between the cracks.

- **Execution Discipline:** End-to-end delivery, no gap without a clear owner
- **Decision Quality:** Trade for impact, accountability, and clarity, not comfort
- **Cultural Behavior:** High trust, direct accountability across peers

### 2. Speed Over Comfort

**Ship fast, learn faster. Perfect is too slow.**

Speed Over Comfort means we value progress over perfection. No waiting for learning plans, updates, and formalities. Because faster cycles make long debates and slow execution even more expensive.

- **Execution Discipline:** Bias to start and finish — just ship it
- **Decision Quality:** Trade decisions with adequate data, expect results
- **Cultural Behavior:** Celebrate progress and learning, not metrics alone

### 3. Impact Obsessed

**Focus only on what moves the metrics that matter.**

Impact Obsessed prioritizes data-to-cash, to measurable outcomes. It ensures the org doesn't chase brand and perceived items towards what truly drives our business, not towards noise, effort, or intent.

- **Execution Discipline:** Make before ideas; resources only go to outcomes
- **Decision Quality:** Prioritize metrics that track growth or profitability
- **Cultural Behavior:** Trade reviews, celebrate results over effort focused

### 4. Simplify to Scale

**Complexity Kills. Simplicity Scales.**

Simplicity is a force multiplier. Complexity slows execution, confuses clarity, and postpones the organizational rhythm and results while Reality, Doubt, Teams stop/disjoint processes, communication, and structure gives space to efficiency.

- **Execution Discipline:** Choose the simplest path that solves 80% of value
- **Decision Quality:** Prioritize clarity, not clever; reduce layers and roles
- **Cultural Behavior:** Fewer meetings, shorter docs, reduce layers and roles

### 5. Disciplined

**Do more with less; constraints breed creativity.**

Discipline is how we ensure quality, consistency, and sustainable growth. It means using resources wisely, maintaining standards, and holding ourselves and each other to a higher bar, even at low cost or no cost.

- **Execution Discipline:** Efficient use of time, capital, and technology
- **Cultural Behavior:** Pride in resourcefulness, smart spending as storytelling

## Operating Principles 2026 FAQ

### How do our Operating Principles come into development?
They emerged directly from the work we've done this past year to overhaul how we operate and innovate. As an individual Company Role and the IGNITE Principles, the same themes keep surfacing in how our best-performing teams and leaders work.

### Why are we introducing Operating Principles now?
Our company is at an inflection point. We need shared, scalable, and precise north-star guardrails to operate and scale. To do so, we need a shared set that defines how we execute, not just what we believe — that's our set-for-all and deliverables gaps but our specific or operational enough for high-performance reality. The Operating Principles give more than a slogan — they're standards.

### What are the values and Operating Principles related?
- Customers' Passion → Impact Obsessed
- With A Focus → Simplify to Scale + Extreme Ownership
- Ethics & Care → Simplify to Scale + Extreme Ownership
- Respect, Integrity & Inclusion → Cultural Behaviors embedded within every Principle. This is not a cultural formality — it's an operating expectation.

### What is the difference between values and Operating Principles?
Values = identity, north star — who we are. Operating Principles are specific, actionable, measurable and tied to individual performance. This is a cultural formality — it is operating.

### Why do we set leaders more measurable/profitable?
Great leadership is:
- results-driven
- people-focused
- unquely done-a need
